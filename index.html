Supplement Protocol
Heads up: These are general wellness supplements used in recovery contexts. Always consult your doctor before starting, especially if you take medications or have health conditions.
Before You Quit — Preparation (1 Week Prior)
🌿
NAC (N-Acetyl Cysteine)
600mg twice daily
Regulates glutamate — the brain chemical that drives compulsive cravings. One of the most evidence-backed supplements for addiction recovery.
🐟
Omega-3 Fish Oil
2,000–3,000mg EPA+DHA daily
Supports brain cell membrane health, reduces inflammation, and improves mood. The brain is 60% fat — feed it well.
🪨
Magnesium Glycinate
300–400mg at night
Calms the nervous system, reduces anxiety, and dramatically improves sleep quality. The glycinate form is gentlest on digestion.
☀️
Vitamin D3 + K2
2,000–5,000 IU D3 + 100mcg K2
Supports mood regulation, immune health, and cognitive function. Most people are deficient, especially those who smoke.
⚡
B-Complex
1 tablet daily with food
B vitamins are essential for neurotransmitter production (serotonin, dopamine). Smoking depletes B12 and folate.
During Withdrawal — Acute Phase (Days 1–30)
☕
L-Theanine
200mg as needed (up to 2x daily)
Produces calm alertness without sedation. Works synergistically with the nervous system to reduce anxiety and agitation without drowsiness.
🌙
Melatonin
0.5–3mg, 30 min before bed
Cannabis suppresses REM sleep. Without it, vivid dreams and insomnia are common. Start low (0.5mg) — more isn't better.
🌱
Ashwagandha (KSM-66)
300–600mg daily
Adaptogen that lowers cortisol, reduces anxiety, and stabilizes mood. Particularly helpful during the stress of early withdrawal.
🦪
Zinc
15–30mg with food
Supports appetite recovery and immune function. Zinc is depleted by stress and is critical for taste/smell (which dulls with heavy use).
💊
Continue all "Before" supplements
NAC, Omega-3, Magnesium, D3, B-Complex
These form the foundation of your recovery stack. Don't skip them during withdrawal — they matter most now.
After — Long-Term Recovery (30+ Days)
🦁
Lion's Mane Mushroom
500–1,000mg daily
Stimulates NGF (nerve growth factor), promoting neuroplasticity and new neural connections. Supports memory, focus, and mood long-term.
🌺
Rhodiola Rosea
200–400mg in the morning
Adaptogen that combats fatigue and brain fog while improving energy and stress resilience. Take in the morning — can be stimulating.
📉
Taper Sleep Aids
Reduce melatonin gradually
As natural sleep patterns restore, you'll need less sleep support. Taper melatonin from 3mg → 1mg → 0.5mg over weeks 4–8.
Craving Strategies
Remember: Cravings peak in 15–20 minutes, then pass. You just have to outlast the wave.
🌊
Urge Surfing
Observe your craving like a surfer watching a wave — without riding it. Cravings are not commands. They're sensations that rise and fall.
  1. Close your eyes and notice where you feel the craving in your body
  2. Describe it — tight chest? restless hands? a pull in your gut?
  3. Breathe into it and watch it shift, change, or soften
  4. Remind yourself: "This is temporary. It will pass."
🖐️
5-4-3-2-1 Grounding
Pulls you out of the craving spiral and back into the present moment using your senses.
  1. Name 5 things you can see
  2. Name 4 things you can touch
  3. Name 3 things you can hear
  4. Name 2 things you can smell
  5. Name 1 thing you can taste
🚨
HALT Check
Many cravings are disguised basic needs. Check in before assuming you "need" to use.
  1. H — Am I Hungry? (eat something)
  2. A — Am I Angry or Anxious? (name the feeling)
  3. L — Am I Lonely? (reach out to someone)
  4. T — Am I Tired? (rest, don't use)
🔲
Box Breathing
Activates the parasympathetic nervous system, reducing anxiety and craving intensity within minutes.
  1. Inhale slowly for 4 counts
  2. Hold for 4 counts
  3. Exhale slowly for 4 counts
  4. Hold for 4 counts
  5. Repeat 4–6 cycles
🏃
Move Your Body
Physical movement burns off restless energy and releases endorphins that dampen cravings. Even 5 minutes works.
  1. Walk briskly around the block
  2. Do 20 jumping jacks or push-ups
  3. Stretch for 5 minutes
  4. Dance to one song
🌡️
Cold Water Reset
Triggers the dive reflex, rapidly slowing heart rate and clearing the mind. Fast and powerful.
  1. Splash cold water on your face
  2. Or hold ice cubes for 30 seconds
  3. Or step outside into cold air
  4. The shock interrupts the craving loop
⏱️
Delay 20 Minutes
Commit to doing nothing for just 20 minutes. Don't say never — just not right now. Most cravings collapse in this window.
  1. Set a 20-minute timer
  2. Choose a distraction: show, game, call
  3. When timer goes off, reassess
  4. Most of the time, the craving will have moved on
📞
Reach Out
Isolation fuels cravings. Connection dissolves them. You don't have to explain — just be with someone.
  1. Text or call someone you trust
  2. Post in a recovery community or Reddit
  3. Tell them how you're feeling right now
  4. Ask them to stay on the line for a bit
✏️
Write It Out
Externalizing the craving by writing it down interrupts automatic thinking and reduces its power.
  1. Grab any paper or open notes
  2. Write: "Right now I'm feeling ____ because ____"
  3. Write what you're afraid will happen if you don't use
  4. Write one thing you're proud of since quitting
EFT Tapping
What is EFT?
Emotional Freedom Technique (EFT) combines gentle tapping on acupressure points with spoken affirmations. Research shows it lowers cortisol, reduces cravings, and calms the amygdala — your brain's alarm system. It can feel strange at first; that's okay. It works.
Step 1 — Setup Statement
Tap the Karate Chop point (outside edge of hand) while saying each phrase aloud 3 times:
"Even though I have this craving right now, I deeply and completely accept myself."

"Even though my body is craving relief, I choose to offer it something better."

"Even though this feels uncomfortable, I am safe, I am healing, and I am enough."
Step 2 — Tapping Sequence

Tap each point 5–7 times while saying the phrase. Repeat the full sequence 2–3 times.

1
Top of Head
Center crown of the skull
"This craving in my body..." / "This urge to escape..."
2
Eyebrow Point
Inner edge of the eyebrow, above the nose
"I'm tired of feeling this way..." / "This anxious feeling..."
3
Side of Eye
Outer corner of the eye, on the bone
"I want relief right now..." / "This restlessness..."
4
Under Eye
Under the pupil, on the cheekbone
"My body remembers the feeling..." / "This pull inside me..."
5
Under Nose
Between nose and upper lip
"Even this craving..." / "This discomfort I'm in..."
6
Chin Point
In the crease between lower lip and chin
"I'm doing the hard thing..." / "I'm choosing differently..."
7
Collarbone Point
Just below and inward from the collarbone notch
"My body is healing..." / "I'm safe without it..."
8
Under Arm
About 4 inches below the armpit
"I release what no longer serves me..." / "I'm stronger than this urge..."
9
Wrist Points
Inner wrists tapped together
"I love and accept myself..." / "Peace. Clarity. Strength."
Step 3 — Closing
Take a deep breath in and release slowly. Rate your craving intensity from 0–10. If still above 4, repeat the sequence with fresh phrases. End with:
"I am healing. I choose myself. My brain is getting clearer every day."

CBT Strategies
🔍
Identify Your Triggers (PPTE)
Foundation
Triggers fall into four categories. Once you know yours, you can plan around them.
  • People — Who do you use with? Who stresses you out?
  • Places — Where do you typically use? Where do you go after?
  • Things — What objects, smells, or times of day cue the urge?
  • Emotions — Boredom? Anxiety? Loneliness? Celebration?
Write your top 3 triggers below. Having them written makes the pattern visible — and visible patterns can be interrupted.
📝
Thought Record (ABC Model)
In-the-moment tool
When a craving hits, use this to slow down automatic thinking.
🧩
Challenge Cognitive Distortions
Thought patterns
Cannabis use is often maintained by predictable thinking traps. Learn to spot them:
  • "Just this once" — Is once ever really once? What does the data say?
  • "I can't handle this sober" — Have you ever handled hard things sober? You likely have.
  • "It's not like it's a real drug" — Addiction is defined by compulsion and consequences, not the substance.
  • "I need it to sleep/focus/relax" — Feels true, but these are withdrawal symptoms, not proof of need.
  • "I've already ruined it" — A slip isn't failure. It's information. Restart doesn't erase progress.
⚖️
Cost-Benefit Analysis
Clarity tool
When you're tempted to use, run this honest accounting in your head (or on paper):

Reset counter

Hiding it

🌱
Values Clarification
Big picture
When cravings make the short-term feel overwhelming, reconnect with the long game.
  • What does freedom from cannabis give you back? (energy, money, clarity, presence)
  • What kind of person do you want to be — and does using align with that?
  • Who is watching you succeed? (even just future-you)
  • Write your "Why I'm quitting" statement and keep it somewhere you'll see it
Reading your values during a craving interrupts the dopamine chase and activates the prefrontal cortex — your wise, long-term thinking brain.
📋
Behavioral Activation
Mood & energy
Cannabis often fills time that feels empty or uncomfortable. Replace it intentionally:
  • Make a personal activity menu: 3 things you can do in 5 min, 3 in 30 min, 3 in 2+ hours
  • Schedule one enjoyable activity per day — don't wait to feel like it
  • Notice the high-risk times (evenings? weekends?) and pre-plan for them
  • Track your mood daily to see the link between activity and feeling better
🛡️
Relapse Prevention Plan
Protect your progress
Build your plan before you need it, not during a crisis.
  • Early warning signs: What thoughts/feelings signal you're at risk?
  • High-risk situations: List the top 3 and plan an exit for each
  • Emergency contact: Name one person to call when things get hard
  • If I slip: No shame spiral. Restart the clock. Ask what I can learn.
  • Reward system: What will I give myself at day 7, 30, 90, 180?
Important: A slip is not a relapse. A relapse is not a failure. Recovery is not linear. What matters is what you do next.
